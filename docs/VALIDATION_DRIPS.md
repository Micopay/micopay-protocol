V-5 · Product validation: trust in the cash-in/cash-out flow

Core Flow Validation: End-to-End Liquidity Provider Experience

    General Region: Africa 

1. Trust Context (Most vs. Least)

    Most Trusted: A well-established, branded neighborhood PoS agent (e.g., Monietravel, Mono) who has a permanent physical kiosk and a steady stream of daily customers. Seeing them use a dedicated, functional Android PoS terminal instantly builds confidence.

    Least Trusted: A mobile, unbranded agent sitting casually on the street corner with just a phone, or an untrusted shop owner who claims "network is bad" before even attempting the transaction.

2. Minimum Required Information Before Handing Over Cash

    The exact commission fee clearly stated upfront (e.g., "Fee: ₦200").

    The final, locked-in Naira value that will reflect in the digital wallet.

    A clear confirmation on the agent's screen showing that the Drips system is active and online before physical cash changes hands.

3. Verification Signals for Providers

    The agent’s location and banner name matching exactly what is displayed on the in-app map directory.

    A verified merchant status indicator within the app, ideally showing a high completion rate or a "Trusted Agent" badge.

4. Support Expectations (Mid-Operation Failure)

    In the event of a network glitch or a "debit/credit hang," an immediate, automated SMS receipt or an in-app "Pending / Money Received" status to prove the transaction is in flight.

    A clear, accessible WhatsApp support channel or a direct toll-free customer care line to resolve hanging transactions within minutes, rather than leaving the user stranded at the kiosk.

5. Top Reason to Abandon the Flow

    "Network Issues" & Delay: If the agent says "network is fluctuated" or if the app takes too long to generate the QR code/receipt. In Nigeria, any delay at the point of payment triggers a fear of trapped funds, making it an immediate reason to cancel and walk away.
